# Luxury Fashion Doctrine
business_type: luxury-fashion
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0B0B0D"
- secondary: "#F5F0E8"
- accent: "#C9A860"
- psychology: Deep noir signals exclusivity and power, ivory conveys heritage refinement, and muted gold whispers wealth without shouting it.

### Typography
- heading: Didot (Serif)
- body: Neue Haas Grotesk (Sans-serif)
- psychology: High-contrast didone headlines evoke couture editorial authority while a restrained grotesque body reads as quiet modern confidence.

### Imagery
- style: Editorial fashion photography with cinematic lighting, muted palettes, and negative space.
- subjects: Single garments or models in architectural settings, macro shots of stitching, ateliers, and materials.
- psychology: Images should trigger aspiration, reverence for craft, and the feeling of entering a private world.

### Layout
- style: Ultra-minimal editorial grid with oversized imagery and sparse text.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Abundant emptiness signals that the brand does not need to compete for attention — scarcity is the message.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Hidden hamburger or hairline top bar with minimal items and no promotional clutter.
- cta_style: Understated text-link CTAs ("Discover", "Enquire") with hairline underlines, never loud buttons.
- psychology: Restraint in interaction design mirrors the discretion of a private client experience.

### Copy Style
- tone: Reserved, poetic, heritage-driven.
- length: Short
- psychology: Few words imply the product speaks for itself; scarcity of language mirrors scarcity of supply.
