# Religious Organizations Doctrine
business_type: religious
sector: Public & Non-Profit

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#3B2C6A"
- secondary: "#F7F2E7"
- accent: "#C9A227"
- psychology: Deep contemplative violet evokes reverence and the sacred, warm ivory reads as peace and welcome, and a soft liturgical gold signals the eternal and the divine without ostentation.

### Typography
- heading: Cormorant Garamond (Serif)
- body: Lora (Serif)
- psychology: Elegant old-style serifs carry the weight of tradition and scripture, making the reader feel connected to something timeless, rooted, and larger than themselves.

### Imagery
- style: Warm, soft-light photography of gathered community, worship, and quiet reflection; symbolism over spectacle.
- subjects: Congregation in fellowship, hands in prayer, light through architecture, service to others, and multi-generational belonging.
- psychology: Images should trigger belonging, peace, and the longing to be part of a caring, enduring community.

### Layout
- style: Calm, generous single-column flow anchored by service times and a clear "plan your visit" path.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Openness and breathing room create a felt sense of sanctuary and lower the anxiety of a first-time visitor.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple welcoming top bar leading with "Visit", "Times", and a persistent "Give" option.
- cta_style: Gentle, invitational buttons ("Plan your visit", "Join us Sunday", "Give online") that feel warm, not transactional.
- psychology: Low-pressure, invitational language honors the emotional weight of choosing to belong and reduces newcomer hesitation.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, welcoming, reverent, and inclusive.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Hospitable first-person plural language ("come as you are", "you belong here") converts spiritual seeking into a decision to show up.
