# Sports & Athletic Teams Doctrine
business_type: sports
sector: Entertainment & Media

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#C8102E"
- secondary: "#1D1D1B"
- accent: "#FFB81C"
- psychology: Aggressive team-jersey red spikes heart rate and rallies loyalty, blackout charcoal projects strength and stadium authority, and championship gold cues victory and reward.

### Typography
- heading: Anton (Sans-serif)
- body: Roboto Condensed (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Heavy compressed varsity display type reads as jersey-number bravado and locker-room intensity while a condensed body packs stats and schedules with athletic efficiency.

### Imagery
- style: High-shutter action photography, roaring-crowd wide shots, and slow-motion hero reels with dramatic contrast.
- subjects: Athletes mid-play, celebration moments, packed stands, trophies, and behind-the-scenes tunnel walks.
- psychology: Images should trigger pride, tribal belonging, and the visceral rush of competition and shared triumph.

### Layout
- style: Bold blocky grid with oversized scores, ticket CTAs, and a stadium-signage energy of stacked panels.
- white_space: Minimal
- psychology: Dense high-energy layouts mirror the sensory overload of game day and keep fans scanning for the next fixture or drop.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top bar with Fixtures, Tickets, Shop, and Live-Score badge always in reach.
- cta_style: Loud full-width buttons with motion ("Get Tickets", "Buy Kit", "Join the Members Club").
- psychology: Urgent scarcity-driven CTAs convert the emotional peak of fandom into immediate action before the moment cools.

### Copy Style
- tone: Rallying, tribal, chest-thumping.
- length: Short
- psychology: Chant-like short copy fuels collective identity and turns readers into a we, not a me.
