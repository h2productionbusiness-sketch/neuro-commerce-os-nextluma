# B2B SaaS Doctrine
business_type: saas-b2b
sector: Technology & SaaS

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1E3A8A"
- secondary: "#0F172A"
- accent: "#38BDF8"
- psychology: Deep enterprise blues signal reliability and low-risk procurement while the sky-blue accent implies modern velocity without startup frivolity.

### Typography
- heading: Inter (Sans-serif)
- body: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Neutral engineered sans-serifs read as documentation-grade competence to technical evaluators and CFO-level buyers.

### Imagery
- style: Clean product-UI screenshots with subtle depth, abstract data-flow illustrations, real workplace photography.
- subjects: Dashboards in context, cross-functional teams reviewing metrics, integration diagrams.
- psychology: Trigger confidence that the tool is already working inside companies like the buyer's.

### Layout
- style: Structured grid with clear section hierarchy, social-proof band above the fold.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Order and breathing room signal a mature vendor who will not create operational chaos.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Mega-menu segmented by solution, role, and industry.
- cta_style: Dual CTA — "Book a demo" primary, "Start free trial" secondary, both benefit-framed.
- psychology: Offering both sales-led and product-led paths respects the buyer's preferred evaluation ritual.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authoritative, ROI-quantified, jargon-fluent but plain.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Buyers defending a budget internally need copy they can paste into a business case.
