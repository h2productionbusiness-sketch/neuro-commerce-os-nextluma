# Gaming Doctrine
business_type: gaming
sector: Entertainment & Media

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0A0E27"
- secondary: "#7B2FF7"
- accent: "#00E5FF"
- psychology: Deep space navy sets an immersive night-mode canvas, electric violet signals fantasy and power fantasy escapism, and neon cyan fires dopamine like a level-up notification.

### Typography
- heading: Rajdhani (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Angular condensed display type reads as high-tech HUD interface authority while a clean neutral body keeps long lore and patch notes legible under fast scrolling.

### Imagery
- style: High-fidelity 3D renders, key art splash frames, and glitch-accented motion loops with saturated rim lighting.
- subjects: Hero characters mid-action, environments, gameplay screenshots, boss reveals, and community fan-art.
- psychology: Images should trigger anticipation, tribal belonging, and the adrenaline of an imminent battle or unlock.

### Layout
- style: Dense card-and-tile grid with hero banners, streamer feeds, and modular content shelves like a storefront console UI.
- white_space: Minimal
- psychology: Packed layouts mirror the abundance of an open-world game and reward exploration, keeping the eye hunting for the next reward.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent bottom or side tab bar with icon-driven hubs (Play, Store, Community, Events) mimicking in-game menus.
- cta_style: High-contrast glowing buttons with hover charge-up animation ("Play Now", "Claim", "Join Server").
- psychology: Game-native interactive feedback makes every click feel like an input that earns a reward.

### Copy Style
- tone: Hype, insider, playfully competitive.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy fragmented copy matches the fast twitch attention of players and speaks the in-group dialect that signals authenticity.
