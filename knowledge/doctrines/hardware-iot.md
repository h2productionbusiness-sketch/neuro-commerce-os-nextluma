# Hardware & IoT Doctrine
business_type: hardware-iot
sector: Technology & SaaS

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A1D24"
- secondary: "#E8EAED"
- accent: "#FF6B35"
- psychology: Industrial graphite communicates engineered durability and premium build quality, cool silver reads as precision hardware, and a bold ember orange draws the eye to the physical product and its connectivity.

### Typography
- heading: Eurostile Extended (Sans-serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A wide techno display evokes machined engineering and industrial design heritage, while a clean body keeps spec-heavy content legible.

### Imagery
- style: Studio product photography, exploded-view renders, in-context lifestyle shots, and connected-device diagrams.
- subjects: The device from multiple angles, in real environments, and app-to-hardware pairing visuals.
- psychology: Images should trigger desire to touch and own the object while proving it works reliably in the real world.

### Layout
- style: Product-hero layout with rotating render, spec tables, use-case sections, and companion-app showcase.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Spacious presentation elevates the hardware to an object of desire and signals confidence in the design.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Clean top nav with Product, Specs, and a prominent "Pre-order" or "Buy now" button.
- cta_style: Strong purchase CTAs with price and shipping confidence ("Pre-order now", "Add to cart").
- psychology: Concrete purchase CTAs plus specs reassure buyers making a tangible, higher-commitment physical purchase.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, precise, product-obsessed.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Specific specs and real-world proof reduce the perceived risk of buying a physical product sight-unseen.
