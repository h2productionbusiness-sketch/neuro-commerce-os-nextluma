# Fashion (general) Doctrine
business_type: fashion
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A1A1A"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#E85D4E"
- psychology: Confident near-black and crisp white create a modern editorial canvas while a vivid coral accent injects the energy and impulse that drives contemporary fashion desire.

### Typography
- heading: Archivo (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A bold condensed display face feels current and trend-forward while a highly legible body keeps product detail and sizing frictionless.

### Imagery
- style: Clean lifestyle and studio lookbook photography with on-model and flat-lay variety.
- subjects: Models wearing full looks, styled outfit combinations, fabric texture close-ups, and street-style context shots.
- psychology: Images should trigger aspiration, self-expression, and the anticipation of how the piece completes the shopper's identity.

### Layout
- style: Modern responsive grid with large product tiles and lookbook editorial breaks.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A rhythmic grid balances discovery browsing with clear merchandising so shoppers keep scrolling and adding.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Category mega-menu with filters, a persistent cart, and quick-add on hover.
- cta_style: High-contrast "Add to Bag" and "Shop the Look" buttons in the coral accent.
- psychology: Fast, decisive shopping interactions capitalize on trend-driven impulse before hesitation sets in.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, contemporary, style-savvy.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy trend-aware copy signals cultural relevance and makes the shopper feel in-the-know.
