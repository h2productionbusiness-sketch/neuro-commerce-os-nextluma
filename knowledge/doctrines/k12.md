# K-12 Schools Doctrine
business_type: k12
sector: Education & Training

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1D6FB8"
- secondary: "#FFF7E6"
- accent: "#F2A93B"
- psychology: A dependable school-blue conveys safety, structure, and institutional trust to protective parents, warm cream softens the tone for children, and a sunny amber accent signals cheer, energy, and nurturing optimism.

### Typography
- heading: Nunito (Sans-serif)
- body: Open Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded soft-terminal headlines feel warm and child-friendly while a plainspoken humanist body reassures busy parents that information is clear and honest.

### Imagery
- style: Warm candid documentary photography of real classrooms and campus life, brightly lit and authentic.
- subjects: Diverse smiling students engaged in learning, caring teachers, safe facilities, and proud milestone moments.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, belonging, and a parent's felt sense that their child will be safe, seen, and thriving.

### Layout
- style: Structured, information-rich layout with clear sections for academics, safety, admissions, and community.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Orderly organization signals a well-run institution and reduces the parental anxiety of an important decision.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Clear top menu with prominent "Apply", "Tour", and "Contact" plus quick links for current families.
- cta_style: Friendly solid buttons emphasizing low-commitment next steps ("Book a Campus Tour", "Request Info").
- psychology: Offering a reversible visit before commitment lowers stakes on a high-emotion family decision.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, reassuring, values-driven.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Language that centers safety, care, and character speaks to a parent's deepest protective instincts before academics.
