# Telecom Doctrine
business_type: telecom
sector: Technology & SaaS

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#004E92"
- secondary: "#F4F6F9"
- accent: "#E4002B"
- psychology: A broad signal-blue conveys nationwide reliability and always-on connection, clean light-grey feels modern and accessible, and a vivid red accent drives urgency around offers and coverage.

### Typography
- heading: DIN Next (Sans-serif)
- body: Open Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A signage-grade sans reads as infrastructural and dependable — the visual language of networks people rely on daily.

### Imagery
- style: Coverage maps, connected-lifestyle photography, network-signal motifs, and bright everyday-usage scenes.
- subjects: People staying connected everywhere, coverage visualizations, and speed/reliability demonstrations.
- psychology: Images should trigger the reassurance of being connected and the mild fear of being left without signal.

### Layout
- style: Plan-comparison layout with coverage checker, tiered pricing tables, and prominent offer banners.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clear side-by-side plan comparison reduces choice anxiety for a commodity buyer comparing bundles.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Top nav with Plans, Coverage, and Deals plus a persistent "Check coverage" or "Shop plans" CTA.
- cta_style: Offer-forward CTAs with price and urgency ("Get this deal", "Switch and save").
- psychology: Price-and-urgency CTAs suit a value-driven buyer motivated by savings and limited-time bundles.

### Copy Style
- tone: Clear, reassuring, deal-driven.
- length: Short
- psychology: Simple, benefit-and-price copy cuts through commodity confusion and makes switching feel effortless.
