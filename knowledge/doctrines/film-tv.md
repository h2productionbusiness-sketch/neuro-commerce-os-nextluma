# Film & TV Production Doctrine
business_type: film-tv
sector: Entertainment & Media

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0C0C0C"
- secondary: "#E50914"
- accent: "#D4AF37"
- psychology: Cinema-black recreates the darkened theater that primes total immersion, streaming red ignites urgency and must-watch desire, and awards gold cues prestige and critical acclaim.

### Typography
- heading: Bebas Neue (Sans-serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Tall cinematic poster type reads as marquee title-card drama while a clean humanist body keeps synopses and credits calmly readable against dark backdrops.

### Imagery
- style: Cinematic key art, letterboxed stills, and trailer-frame stills with dramatic color grading and lens flare.
- subjects: Hero cast portraits, tension-filled scene stills, behind-the-scenes set shots, and title treatments.
- psychology: Images should trigger anticipation, emotional intrigue, and the fear of missing the cultural conversation.

### Layout
- style: Poster-forward hero layout with horizontal content rails and autoplay hover previews like a streaming home screen.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Cinematic negative space around key art lets the title breathe as an event while rails invite endless discovery.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Minimal top bar (Watch, Trailers, Cast, News) with a persistent Play or Watchlist action.
- cta_style: Bold play-triangle and Watch Now buttons with subtle glow, plus Add to Watchlist secondary actions.
- psychology: A single dominant Play CTA lowers decision friction and channels intrigue straight into viewing.

### Copy Style
- tone: Evocative, cinematic, cliffhanger-driven.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Logline-style copy sells the emotional premise in one breath and leaves an open loop the viewer must resolve by watching.
