business_type: mental-health

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#5B6EC4"
- secondary: "#F6F4F1"
- accent: "#E8B4A0"
- psychology: Soft periwinkle-blue calms the nervous system and signals safety, warm off-white lowers clinical coldness, and a muted terracotta accent adds human warmth without alarm.

### Typography
- heading: Recoleta (Serif)
- body: Source Sans 3 (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A gentle rounded serif feels human and unhurried while a plain, highly legible body face reduces cognitive load for anxious readers.

### Imagery
- style: Soft-focus natural lifestyle photography with calm palettes, real emotion, and unposed moments.
- subjects: People in quiet reflection, safe home spaces, hands and warm light, therapists in relaxed non-clinical settings.
- psychology: Images should trigger a sense of safety, being understood, and relief that help feels approachable rather than intimidating.

### Layout
- style: Calm single-column flow with breathing room and gentle sectioning.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Ample space lowers overwhelm and signals that this is a slow, safe place where nothing is demanded too fast.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple, minimal navigation with a prominent, low-pressure "Get matched" or "Book a free consult" path.
- cta_style: Soft, reassuring CTAs ("Talk to someone today", "Start when you're ready") with no urgency countdowns.
- psychology: Removing pressure and choice-overload helps a vulnerable, ambivalent visitor take the first step.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, validating, non-judgmental, plain-language.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Empathic first-person language mirrors the client's feelings so they feel heard before being asked to act.
