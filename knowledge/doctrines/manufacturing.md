# Manufacturing Doctrine
business_type: manufacturing
sector: Other Verticals

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1F2A38"
- secondary: "#D5D8DC"
- accent: "#F26522"
- psychology: Industrial steel-navy conveys engineering rigor and durability, machined-gray signals precision, and safety-orange commands attention and communicates operational competence.

### Typography
- heading: Rajdhani (Sans-serif)
- body: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Squared technical headlines feel engineered and precise while a neutral corporate body reads as spec-sheet reliable to procurement-minded buyers.

### Imagery
- style: Clean industrial photography with sharp focus on machinery, tolerances, and process.
- subjects: Production lines, CNC detail, QA inspection, certifications, and finished components in context.
- psychology: Images should trigger confidence in capability, precision, and scale-readiness.

### Layout
- style: Structured grid with spec tables, capability matrices, and certification badges up front.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Dense but organized information signals depth of capability that B2B buyers use to de-risk sourcing.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Utility-first mega nav (Capabilities, Industries, Certifications, Request Quote) built for evaluators.
- cta_style: Firm rectangular CTAs ("Request a Quote", "Download Spec Sheet") emphasizing action and documentation.
- psychology: Efficient, information-rich interaction respects engineers and buyers comparing suppliers on facts.

### Copy Style
- tone: Technical, authoritative, benefit-and-spec driven.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Precise claims backed by tolerances, certifications, and lead times build supplier credibility.
