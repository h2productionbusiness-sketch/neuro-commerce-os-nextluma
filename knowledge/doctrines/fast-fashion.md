# Fast Fashion Doctrine
business_type: fast-fashion
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#FF2E63"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#FFD60A"
- psychology: Hot magenta triggers impulse and youthful excitement, crisp white keeps the grid scannable at speed, and electric yellow flags urgency on price tags and countdowns.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded geometric headlines feel friendly and fast-scrolling while a neutral body keeps dense catalog pages legible on tiny phone screens.

### Imagery
- style: Bright flat-lit lifestyle and UGC-style phone photography with high color saturation.
- subjects: Relatable young models, outfit-of-the-day flat lays, mirror selfies, and haul unboxings.
- psychology: Images should trigger joy, "I could pull that off" relatability, and the anticipation of a cheap-thrill purchase.

### Layout
- style: Dense scrollable product grid with sticky filters and stacked promo banners.
- white_space: Minimal
- psychology: Packed grids create abundance and endless-scroll dopamine, signaling there is always one more deal to find.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent bottom tab bar plus mega-category menu optimized for thumb reach.
- cta_style: Loud filled buttons ("Add to Bag", "Shop the Drop") with urgency chips and price-slash badges.
- psychology: Frictionless one-tap buying and visible discounts collapse the gap between desire and checkout.

### Copy Style
- tone: Playful, trend-slang, hype-driven.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy trend-coded copy mirrors the speed of the scroll and fuels fear of missing the moment.
