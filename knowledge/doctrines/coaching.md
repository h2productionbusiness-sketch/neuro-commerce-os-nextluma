# Coaching (general) Doctrine
business_type: coaching
sector: Education & Training

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#2A2438"
- secondary: "#F7F3EE"
- accent: "#E0A458"
- psychology: Grounded plum-charcoal conveys depth and personal authority, warm off-white feels safe and human, and golden amber signals warmth, transformation, and the promise of a brighter future self.

### Typography
- heading: Fraunces (Serif)
- body: Work Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: An expressive, warm serif conveys the coach's personal voice and authenticity while a clean sans keeps the promise feeling approachable rather than corporate.

### Imagery
- style: Authentic personal-brand photography with natural light and genuine expression.
- subjects: The coach on camera, client transformation moments, candid speaking, and lifestyle-aspiration scenes.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, aspiration, and the emotional pull of a relatable mentor who has walked the path.

### Layout
- style: Story-driven long-form layout with personal narrative, proof, and a single clear offer.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: A guided, story-first flow builds the parasocial trust required to sell a high-ticket, personal transformation.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Minimal nav focused on one core offer, with a strong sticky call-to-action for a call or free resource.
- cta_style: Warm, direct invitation buttons ("Book a Free Call", "Start Your Transformation", "Apply to Work With Me").
- psychology: A single dominant CTA channels attention toward the one conversion event that matters — the discovery call.

### Copy Style
- tone: Personal, empathetic, empowering, first-person and direct.
- length: Long
- psychology: Long emotionally resonant storytelling builds the deep trust and self-identification needed to justify premium coaching investment.
