# General E-commerce Doctrine
business_type: ecommerce
sector: Retail & E-commerce

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A73E8"
- secondary: "#F4F6F9"
- accent: "#FF6A00"
- psychology: Trust-blue anchors payment confidence, a clean cool-grey canvas reduces cognitive load during browsing, and a high-energy orange accent drives urgency on add-to-cart moments.

### Typography
- heading: Inter (Sans-serif)
- body: Roboto (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Neutral, highly legible geometric sans fonts read as modern and frictionless, keeping attention on product and price rather than the interface.

### Imagery
- style: Bright, high-key product photography on clean backgrounds with lifestyle context shots.
- subjects: Products from multiple angles, in-use lifestyle scenes, size and scale references, and customer UGC.
- psychology: Images should trigger anticipation of ownership, trust in quality, and confidence that what you see is what you get.

### Layout
- style: Conversion-optimized grid with prominent product cards, sticky cart, and clear filtering.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Scannable density lets shoppers compare quickly while breathing room around CTAs reduces friction at the decision point.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent search bar, mega-menu categories, and a sticky cart with item count.
- cta_style: Bold high-contrast "Add to Cart" and "Buy Now" buttons with urgency and stock cues.
- psychology: Familiar, predictable commerce patterns lower purchase anxiety and speed the path to checkout.

### Copy Style
- tone: Clear, benefit-driven, and reassuring.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Concrete benefits plus social proof and guarantees reduce perceived risk and justify the click to buy.
