# EdTech (Online Courses) Doctrine
business_type: edtech
sector: Education & Training

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#4C3AE3"
- secondary: "#F4F6FB"
- accent: "#22C55E"
- psychology: Electric indigo signals modern intelligence and momentum, a clean cool-white canvas reduces cognitive load for learners, and a growth-green accent flags progress and "you completed it" reward moments.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Friendly geometric headlines feel approachable and beginner-safe while a highly legible neutral body keeps long lessons frictionless and non-intimidating.

### Imagery
- style: Bright, optimistic lifestyle photography mixed with clean UI screenshots and outcome infographics.
- subjects: Real learners at laptops, before/after skill proof, dashboards showing progress, and diverse relatable faces mid-breakthrough.
- psychology: Images should trigger aspiration, self-efficacy, and the belief that transformation is achievable for someone like me.

### Layout
- style: Modular card-based grid with clear step ladders, progress bars, and scannable module breakdowns.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Structured chunking reassures the anxious learner that the material is finite, sequenced, and conquerable.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top nav with a persistent "Enroll" button and anchor jumps to curriculum, outcomes, and testimonials.
- cta_style: High-contrast rounded buttons with action-outcome copy ("Start Learning Free", "Get Instant Access").
- psychology: Removing friction and promising immediacy converts the impulse spike before doubt returns.

### Copy Style
- tone: Encouraging, credible, outcome-obsessed.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Concrete result-driven language sells the transformation, not the lectures, so the buyer pictures their after-state.
