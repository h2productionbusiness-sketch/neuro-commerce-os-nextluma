# Nutrition & Supplements Doctrine
business_type: nutrition
sector: Health & Wellness

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1B7A4C"
- secondary: "#F4F1E9"
- accent: "#F2A03D"
- psychology: Vital leaf-green signals natural potency and clean sourcing, warm oat-cream reads as wholesome and non-clinical, and amber-turmeric accents cue energy and bioavailable nutrition.

### Typography
- heading: Sofia Pro (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded geometric headlines feel approachable and health-forward while a neutral body face keeps ingredient claims legible and trustworthy.

### Imagery
- style: Bright natural-light macro and lifestyle photography with clean surfaces and real whole-food ingredients.
- subjects: Powders and capsules beside raw ingredients, active people mid-routine, supplement-fact clarity, and hands preparing shakes.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust in purity, anticipation of feeling better, and aspiration toward a stronger self.

### Layout
- style: Modular ingredient-led grid with prominent benefit callouts and comparison blocks.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Structured whitespace makes efficacy claims feel scientific and scannable, lowering skepticism about what is inside.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top bar with benefit-based categories and a visible subscribe-and-save entry point.
- cta_style: High-contrast benefit CTAs ("Start Your Stack", "Subscribe & Save 20%") with trust badges nearby.
- psychology: Persistent subscription cues frame supplementation as an ongoing habit rather than a one-time purchase.

### Copy Style
- tone: Evidence-led, energetic, reassuring.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Balancing claims with cited backing satisfies the health-anxious buyer who wants both hope and proof.
