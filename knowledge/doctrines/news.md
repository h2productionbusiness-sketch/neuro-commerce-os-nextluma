# News & Journalism Doctrine
business_type: news
sector: Entertainment & Media

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A1A1A"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#B31942"
- psychology: Newsprint black-on-white signals objectivity and unfiltered fact, while a masthead crimson accent flags urgency and breaking authority without overwhelming credibility.

### Typography
- heading: Libre Franklin (Sans-serif)
- body: Georgia (Serif)
- psychology: A sturdy grotesque headline face reads as trustworthy front-page authority while a classic reading serif signals editorial gravitas and comfortable long-form legibility.

### Imagery
- style: Documentary photojournalism with candid, high-realism framing and minimal retouching.
- subjects: On-the-ground events, real people and places, data charts, and reporter bylines.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, urgency, and the felt sense of being present at real events as they happen.

### Layout
- style: Dense newspaper grid with a lead story, headline hierarchy, section columns, and timestamped feed.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A structured hierarchy of headlines mirrors a front page, guiding the eye to importance and signaling comprehensive coverage.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Section-tab top bar (World, Politics, Business, Live) with a sticky breaking-news banner and search.
- cta_style: Clear text and outline CTAs ("Read the full story", "Subscribe", "Get the briefing") with subscription paywall prompts.
- psychology: Straightforward, no-hype CTAs preserve editorial credibility while urgency banners drive the impulse to stay informed.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authoritative, precise, urgent-but-measured.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Inverted-pyramid clarity front-loads the fact and builds trust, while a sharp headline creates the curiosity gap that earns the click.
