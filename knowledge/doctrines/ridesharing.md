# Ride-Sharing & Mobility Doctrine
business_type: ridesharing
sector: Automotive & Transport

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#141414"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#5C5CFF"
- psychology: Crisp black-on-white signals modern app-native simplicity and premium neutrality, while electric indigo-blue conveys motion, technology, and a friendly always-available urban energy.

### Typography
- heading: Manrope (Sans-serif)
- body: Manrope (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A clean rounded geometric family feels approachable, mobile-first, and effortlessly modern, matching a tap-and-go experience.

### Imagery
- style: Bright lifestyle photography with real riders, city motion blur, and clean map-UI overlays.
- subjects: People getting in and out of vehicles, drivers earning, live map pins, and everyday urban journeys.
- psychology: Images should trigger the joy of convenience, belonging in a connected city, and trust that a ride is seconds away.

### Layout
- style: Mobile-first stacked layout with map hero, single-action cards, and thumb-friendly spacing.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Spacious single-focus screens reduce cognitive load so the next tap always feels obvious and instant.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Bottom-tab or map-first flow with one dominant action per screen.
- cta_style: Full-width pill buttons ("Request Ride", "Get Moving", "Start Earning") in bold indigo.
- psychology: A single high-contrast action per screen removes friction and delivers the instant gratification riders expect.

### Copy Style
- tone: Friendly, energetic, effortless and human.
- length: Short
- psychology: Brief upbeat language matches on-the-go micro-decisions and makes signing up or riding feel frictionless.
