# Government & Public Sector Doctrine
business_type: government
sector: Public & Non-Profit

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#12345B"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#B22234"
- psychology: Deep civic navy conveys stability, authority, and trust, clean white signals transparency and neutrality, and a restrained flag-red accent marks official action without partisan tone.

### Typography
- heading: Public Sans (Sans-serif)
- body: Public Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A single accessible, government-grade sans-serif reads as official, neutral, and impartial while meeting the clarity standard citizens expect from public institutions.

### Imagery
- style: Clean, high-accessibility photography of civic settings, infrastructure, and diverse citizens; iconography over decoration.
- subjects: Public services in action, diverse residents, civic buildings, staff serving the community, and clear wayfinding icons.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, a sense of fairness, and confidence that services are competent and available to everyone.

### Layout
- style: Structured, WCAG-compliant grid with clear task hierarchy and plain-language section headers.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Predictable, accessible structure signals competence and reduces citizen anxiety when completing high-stakes official tasks.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent utility navigation with breadcrumb trails and a prominent search bar for task-finding.
- cta_style: Clear, verb-first buttons ("Apply now", "Check status", "Pay online") with obvious focus states.
- psychology: Unambiguous, task-oriented controls build trust that the citizen will complete their goal without error or hidden steps.

### Copy Style
- tone: Plain, neutral, authoritative, and reassuring.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Plain language at a low reading level maximizes comprehension and equity, signaling the institution serves all citizens fairly.
