# Test Prep & Tutoring Doctrine
business_type: test-prep
sector: Education & Training

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1B3A8A"
- secondary: "#FFF9E6"
- accent: "#FF8A3D"
- psychology: Confident academic blue signals reliability and score authority, warm cream softens exam anxiety, and energetic orange creates urgency and motivation to act before the test date.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Nunito Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded, friendly geometric type lowers the intimidation of high-stakes testing while staying crisp enough to feel organized and trustworthy to results-focused parents.

### Imagery
- style: Bright, upbeat photography and clean data illustration with visible score gains.
- subjects: Focused students, celebratory acceptance moments, tutors at whiteboards, and score-improvement charts.
- psychology: Images should trigger hope, relief from anxiety, and the anticipation of a life-changing result.

### Layout
- style: Conversion-focused layout leading with score guarantees, proof, and clear program tiers.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Proof-forward structure quickly answers the parent's core question — will this actually raise the score — reducing hesitation.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple top nav by test (SAT, ACT, GRE) and format (1:1, group, self-paced) with a sticky enroll CTA.
- cta_style: Urgent, benefit-led buttons ("Book Free Assessment", "Start My Prep", "Claim My Score Boost") in accent color.
- psychology: Free-assessment CTAs lower the entry barrier and let the diagnostic sell the paid program.

### Copy Style
- tone: Encouraging, confident, results- and deadline-driven.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy, reassuring copy with concrete score claims counters test anxiety and converts under deadline pressure.
