# Automotive Dealerships Doctrine
business_type: automotive
sector: Automotive & Transport

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0B1F3A"
- secondary: "#C0C5CC"
- accent: "#D62828"
- psychology: Deep midnight navy signals institutional trust for a five-figure purchase, metallic silver echoes showroom chrome and machine precision, and a controlled red accent injects the urgency and adrenaline that closes test-drive appointments.

### Typography
- heading: Titillium Web (Sans-Serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-Serif)
- psychology: Engineered, slightly angular headings evoke speedometer dials and performance badging while a neutral body face keeps financing details feeling honest and legible.

### Imagery
- style: High-gloss hero photography with dramatic low-angle lighting and motion-blur backgrounds.
- subjects: Three-quarter front vehicle shots, keys-in-hand delivery moments, families loading trunks, detail macro shots of grilles and wheels.
- psychology: Trigger ownership fantasy and status projection first, then reassure with human delivery moments that make the dealership feel like the safe place to sign.

### Layout
- style: Full-bleed hero with vehicle inventory grid below, sticky finance calculator sidebar.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: The car dominates the frame like a showroom floor while structured inventory grids satisfy the comparison-shopping brain before it visits a rival lot.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Mega-menu by body style (SUV, Truck, Sedan) with New/Used/Finance top-level split.
- cta_style: High-contrast red buttons — "Book a Test Drive" and "Get My Trade-In Value" — with payment-per-month framing.
- psychology: Monthly-payment anchoring lowers sticker shock and the test-drive CTA converts online research into the in-person moment where deals actually close.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, spec-literate, locally rooted — the trusted advisor who talks horsepower and APR in the same breath.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Buyers spend weeks researching, so copy must respect their knowledge while quietly resolving the fear of being ripped off by "the dealership."
