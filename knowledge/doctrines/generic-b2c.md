# Generic B2C Doctrine
business_type: generic-b2c
sector: Generic

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#FF5A5F"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#FFB400"
- psychology: A warm energetic coral sparks impulse and approachability, a clean white field keeps products the hero and feels friendly, and a sunny amber accent triggers optimism and urgency at the moment of purchase.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Nunito Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded, geometric letterforms feel approachable and modern, lowering emotional guard and making the brand feel likeable and easy to buy from.

### Imagery
- style: Bright lifestyle photography and vibrant product shots with real people and authentic moments.
- subjects: Happy customers using the product, aspirational everyday scenes, close-up product hero shots.
- psychology: Images should trigger joy, aspiration, and instant desire to belong to the moment shown.

### Layout
- style: Conversion-focused stacked sections with bold hero, social proof, and repeated clear CTAs.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A guided, scannable flow reduces friction and keeps the shopper moving toward the buy button on any device.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple mobile-first top bar with sticky cart and prominent search.
- cta_style: Bold high-contrast buttons ("Shop Now", "Get Yours") with urgency and scarcity cues.
- psychology: Loud, obvious, thumb-friendly actions capitalize on impulse and remove hesitation from the mobile purchase path.

### Copy Style
- tone: Friendly, upbeat, benefit- and emotion-driven.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy, emotional copy triggers fast System-1 decisions and makes buying feel effortless and rewarding.
