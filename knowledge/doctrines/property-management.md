# Property Management Doctrine
business_type: property-management
sector: Real Estate & Construction

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#2C6E63"
- secondary: "#F4F6F3"
- accent: "#F2A541"
- psychology: Grounded teal-green signals dependable stewardship and calm, soft off-white conveys clean well-kept spaces, and warm amber cues responsive helpful service that keeps tenants and owners at ease.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded geometric headlines feel approachable and organized while a legible humanist body signals clear, no-surprises communication.

### Imagery
- style: Bright natural-light photography of tidy interiors, smiling maintenance teams, and happy residents.
- subjects: Well-maintained units, keys and portals, on-site staff resolving requests, and owner dashboards.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, relief, and the reassurance that the property and its people are being cared for.

### Layout
- style: Clean dashboard-inspired grid with service tiers, portal previews, and owner/tenant split paths.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Organized, breathable layouts reassure owners that operations are systematized and nothing falls through the cracks.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Dual-audience top nav splitting Owners and Residents with a prominent portal login.
- cta_style: Friendly solid buttons ("Get a Free Rental Analysis", "Report a Maintenance Issue") with fast-response promises.
- psychology: Clear low-friction CTAs reduce the anxiety owners feel about handing over their asset.

### Copy Style
- tone: Reassuring, transparent, service-oriented.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Plain transparent language builds the trust required for owners to delegate control of a major asset.
