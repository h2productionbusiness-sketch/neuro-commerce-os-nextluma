# Streetwear & Urban Doctrine
business_type: streetwear
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#111111"
- secondary: "#EDEDED"
- accent: "#FF4D00"
- psychology: Blacked-out base reads as underground and authentic, concrete grey nods to urban texture, and a molten orange hit signals hype and limited-edition heat.

### Typography
- heading: Archivo Black (Sans-serif)
- body: Space Grotesk (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Heavy condensed headlines carry graffiti-poster boldness while a technical mono-leaning body feels insider and design-literate.

### Imagery
- style: Gritty street photography, flash-lit lookbooks, and behind-the-scenes drop footage.
- subjects: Crews in city settings, close-ups of graphics and stitching, skate and music culture, sold-out racks.
- psychology: Images should trigger belonging to a scene, insider status, and the tension of scarcity.

### Layout
- style: Bold grid with oversized lockup type, editorial lookbook blocks, and a visible drop calendar.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Confident spacing plus a countdown-driven layout frames the brand as a cultural event, not a shop.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Minimal top bar with a prominent "Drops" entry and a raffle or waitlist gate.
- cta_style: High-contrast blocky buttons ("Cop Now", "Join the Raffle") with stock and timer indicators.
- psychology: Gated access and live scarcity signals turn buying into a competitive, status-earning act.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, culture-fluent, understated flex.
- length: Short
- psychology: Insider slang and few words separate real heads from tourists, reinforcing tribe membership.
