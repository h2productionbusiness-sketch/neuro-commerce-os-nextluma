# Non-Profits & NGOs Doctrine
business_type: nonprofit
sector: Public & Non-Profit

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1F6F5C"
- secondary: "#F4EDE1"
- accent: "#E5883A"
- psychology: Grounded forest-teal signals stewardship and hope, warm parchment reads as human and approachable, and an earthy amber accent sparks the urgency to give without feeling corporate.

### Typography
- heading: Sentinel (Serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A humanist slab-serif headline conveys warmth and moral authority while an open, legible body font makes mission storytelling feel honest and accessible to every reader.

### Imagery
- style: Documentary, natural-light photography of real beneficiaries and volunteers, never stock or staged.
- subjects: Faces mid-story, hands working, communities served, and volunteers in the field with dignity and eye contact.
- psychology: Images should trigger empathy, hope, and the felt sense that one person's donation changes a specific life.

### Layout
- style: Story-led single-column flow with prominent impact numbers and a persistent donate anchor.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clear linear storytelling guides the reader from problem to hope to action without cognitive friction that stalls giving.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple top bar with a high-contrast persistent "Donate" button that never scrolls away.
- cta_style: Warm, benefit-framed buttons ("Give hope today", "Fund a meal") with suggested-amount chips.
- psychology: Reducing decisions and keeping the ask always visible converts fleeting emotion into completed donations.

### Copy Style
- tone: Sincere, hopeful, story-driven, and transparent.
- length: Medium
- psychology: A single named story with a concrete outcome outperforms statistics because donors give to identifiable individuals, not abstractions.
