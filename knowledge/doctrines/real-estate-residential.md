# Residential Real Estate Doctrine
business_type: real-estate-residential
sector: Real Estate & Construction

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1F3A5F"
- secondary: "#F7F3EC"
- accent: "#C9A26B"
- psychology: Deep hearth-navy signals stability for the largest purchase of a lifetime, warm ivory evokes move-in-ready comfort, and soft brass hints at attainable aspiration without luxury intimidation.

### Typography
- heading: Playfair Display (Serif)
- body: Source Sans 3 (Sans-serif)
- psychology: An elegant serif whispers "home with character" while a clean humanist body keeps mortgage-anxious buyers feeling guided, not sold.

### Imagery
- style: Golden-hour lifestyle photography with shallow depth of field; real families, never sterile staging.
- subjects: Front porches at dusk, keys in hand at the threshold, kids in backyards, kitchen gatherings.
- psychology: Trigger the "this is where my life happens" projection so buyers emotionally move in before they financially commit.

### Layout
- style: Airy editorial grid with full-bleed hero listings and generous property cards.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Breathing room mirrors the spaciousness buyers crave and lowers decision anxiety on a high-stakes purchase.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top nav with neighborhood/price-range mega-search front and center.
- cta_style: Warm brass buttons with ownership language ("Tour This Home", "See Yourself Here").
- psychology: Every click should feel like a step toward the front door, not a step into a sales funnel.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, reassuring neighbor-who-knows-the-market.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Emotional storytelling about life in the home paired with calm factual anchors (schools, commute, equity) soothes the fear of overpaying.
