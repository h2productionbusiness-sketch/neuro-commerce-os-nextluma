# IT & Tech Services Doctrine
business_type: it-services
sector: B2B & Professional Services

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0D1B2A"
- secondary: "#F2F5F7"
- accent: "#00B4D8"
- psychology: Dark technical navy signals security, stability, and enterprise-grade seriousness, clean neutral grey reads as precise and uncluttered, and a bright cyan accent conveys innovation, uptime, and connected reliability.

### Typography
- heading: Space Grotesk (Sans-serif)
- body: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A modern technical display voice signals engineering competence while a highly legible systems body reads as trustworthy, precise, and infrastructure-grade.

### Imagery
- style: Clean technical photography, isometric system diagrams, dashboards, and abstract network visuals.
- subjects: Data centers, cloud architecture diagrams, security dashboards, and engineers at work.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, competence, and reassurance that systems are secure and expertly managed.

### Layout
- style: Structured grid with solution blocks, integration logos, and technical proof modules.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clean, systematic structure signals reliability and reduces the perceived risk of a mission-critical partnership.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent top navigation organized by services, industries, and a resources/documentation hub.
- cta_style: Confident solid-button CTAs ("Book a Consultation", "Get a Security Assessment") reinforced by trust badges and certifications.
- psychology: Direct, proof-backed interaction design signals dependability that risk-averse IT buyers require.

### Copy Style
- tone: Clear, credible, technically fluent.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Precise, jargon-appropriate language reassures technical evaluators that the provider truly understands the stack.
