# Healthcare (general) Doctrine
business_type: healthcare
sector: Health & Wellness

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#2C6EAE"
- secondary: "#F5F8FB"
- accent: "#4FC1A6"
- psychology: Dependable medical blue conveys competence and calm authority, soft blue-white reads as clean and orderly, and a healing green accent signals wellbeing and recovery.

### Typography
- heading: Work Sans (Sans-serif)
- body: Roboto (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A steady, humanist sans headline feels competent yet approachable while a workhorse body face keeps care information clear and inclusive.

### Imagery
- style: Warm, authentic documentary-style photography of real caregivers and patients in genuine settings.
- subjects: Clinicians with patients, welcoming reception areas, diverse people receiving care, and reassuring hands-on moments.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, a sense of being cared for, and confidence in competent, compassionate treatment.

### Layout
- style: Clear service-oriented layout with easy wayfinding, proof, and prominent contact and booking paths.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Intuitive structure reduces stress for people navigating care while sick or worried and speeds them to action.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Accessible top navigation organized by service and audience with visible "Book" and phone options.
- cta_style: Clear supportive CTAs ("Find care", "Book an appointment", "Talk to us") with accessibility in mind.
- psychology: Straightforward, accessible CTAs serve a broad, often anxious audience that values ease over cleverness.

### Copy Style
- tone: Compassionate, clear, trustworthy, inclusive.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Plain, empathetic language reassures diverse patients and reduces the fear that surrounds healthcare decisions.
