# Accessories Doctrine
business_type: accessories
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#2B2A33"
- secondary: "#F7F3EE"
- accent: "#D98E5A"
- psychology: Soft charcoal grounds the product as a considered finishing touch, warm cream flatters materials, and a terracotta accent adds tactile, gift-worthy warmth.

### Typography
- heading: Fraunces (Serif)
- body: Karla (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A characterful modern serif adds crafted charm while a clean geometric body keeps the many small details tidy and shoppable.

### Imagery
- style: Detail-rich still life and on-body close-ups with warm natural light and styled props.
- subjects: Bags, belts, scarves, and eyewear styled on-body and as flat lays, texture and hardware macros, gifting scenes.
- psychology: Images should trigger the desire to complete a look, tactile want, and gift-giving warmth.

### Layout
- style: Product-forward grid with styling-suggestion blocks and gift-guide sections.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clean framing lets small hero items feel elevated while styling cues drive add-on desire.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Category and occasion-based nav with a "complete the look" and gifting entry.
- cta_style: Warm rounded buttons ("Add the Finishing Touch", "Gift This") with easy add-on prompts.
- psychology: Low-commitment, add-on framing turns accessories into effortless impulse and gift buys.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, styling-savvy, gift-forward.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Styling and gifting language reframes a small item as the detail that makes the whole outfit.
