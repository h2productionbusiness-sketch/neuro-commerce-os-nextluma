# Fitness & Gyms Doctrine
business_type: fitness
sector: Health & Wellness

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0D0D0D"
- secondary: "#FF3B1F"
- accent: "#C6FF2E"
- psychology: Charcoal black signals discipline and grit while high-voltage red-orange and volt green fire adrenaline, urgency, and physical intensity in transformation-seeking buyers.

### Typography
- heading: Anton (Display Sans)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Condensed, heavyweight headlines feel like a coach shouting effort while a clean grotesque body keeps programs and pricing legible.

### Imagery
- style: High-contrast dramatic photography with sweat, chalk dust, and hard gym lighting.
- subjects: Real members mid-rep, before/after transformations, group classes at peak effort, coaches spotting.
- psychology: Images must trigger "that could be me" identity projection and the visceral thrill of exertion.

### Layout
- style: Bold asymmetric blocks with diagonal cuts and oversized stat callouts.
- white_space: Minimal
- psychology: Dense, energetic composition mirrors the intensity of a workout and keeps arousal high through the scroll.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top bar with a persistent "Claim Free Trial" button.
- cta_style: Full-width high-contrast buttons with action verbs ("Start Training", "Book Your First Session").
- psychology: Commitment-device CTAs convert bursts of motivation before willpower fades.

### Copy Style
- tone: Direct, motivational, coach-like, second person.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy imperative copy mimics coaching cues and converts emotional spikes into immediate action.
