# DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) Doctrine
business_type: dtc
sector: Retail & E-commerce

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#111827"
- secondary: "#FBEEE6"
- accent: "#E85D4E"
- psychology: Confident near-black conveys a premium modern brand, a warm blush backdrop feels personal and human, and a coral accent injects the personality and boldness DTC challengers use to stand apart from legacy incumbents.

### Typography
- heading: Clash Display (Sans-serif)
- body: General Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A characterful display face signals a brand with a point of view while a clean workhorse body keeps education and storytelling effortless to read.

### Imagery
- style: Founder-led, editorial-lifestyle photography mixed with authentic UGC and product-hero shots.
- subjects: Real customers, founder moments, ingredient or material transparency, and before/after outcomes.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust through authenticity, aspiration toward the lifestyle, and the feeling of buying from real people, not a faceless brand.

### Layout
- style: Story-driven long-scroll landing pages with clear value props, proof, and a single focused CTA.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: A guided narrative scroll walks a cold visitor from problem to proof to purchase, controlling the story the way retail shelves never could.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Slim nav with a single dominant hero CTA and social proof surfaced early.
- cta_style: One repeated high-contrast primary CTA ("Shop the Set", "Start Now") reinforced down the page.
- psychology: A single, repeated action removes decision paralysis and channels all momentum toward one conversion.

### Copy Style
- tone: Bold, conversational, and mission-driven.
- length: Long
- psychology: A strong founder voice and a clear "why we exist" build brand allegiance that turns first purchases into identity and repeat loyalty.
