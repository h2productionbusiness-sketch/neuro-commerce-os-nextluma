# B2C SaaS Doctrine
business_type: saas-b2c
sector: Technology & SaaS

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#5B4DF5"
- secondary: "#12122B"
- accent: "#22D3A8"
- psychology: Electric indigo signals modern optimism and product energy, deep ink grounds it in reliability, and mint green flags "go" moments that pull users toward activation.

### Typography
- heading: Inter (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A single clean geometric sans reads as friendly, frictionless, and screen-native — the interface itself feels like the brand.

### Imagery
- style: Bright product screenshots, animated UI mockups, and playful 3D illustration on soft gradients.
- subjects: The app in action, before/after transformations, happy individual users on phones and laptops.
- psychology: Images should trigger anticipation and the "that's me" recognition that makes a free trial feel inevitable.

### Layout
- style: Modular scroll landing with hero demo, benefit blocks, social proof, and repeated sign-up CTAs.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clear rhythmic sections reduce cognitive load so the user always knows the next step is to try it free.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky top bar with product, pricing, and a persistent high-contrast "Start free" button.
- cta_style: Bold filled buttons with action verbs ("Try it free", "Get started") and no credit-card friction copy.
- psychology: Constant frictionless CTAs exploit low commitment thresholds to convert curiosity into an account.

### Copy Style
- tone: Friendly, benefit-led, conversational.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Plain-language outcome copy lowers perceived effort and makes the value feel achievable in minutes.
