## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#3B5A2B"
- secondary: "#E8DCC0"
- accent: "#D98A2B"
- psychology: Deep field green signals growth and living soil, wheat-straw cream conveys harvest abundance, and harvest-orange sparks the warmth of ripe yield and seasonal renewal.

### Typography
- heading: Bitter (Slab-serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Sturdy slab headlines feel grounded and dependable while a plain workhorse body reads honest and unpretentious, matching a hands-on rural buyer.

### Imagery
- style: Natural-light documentary photography with earthy tones and wide horizons.
- subjects: Fields at golden hour, weathered hands in soil, livestock, equipment in use, and farm families.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust in provenance, pride of stewardship, and nostalgic connection to the land.

### Layout
- style: Practical block layout with clear sections and prominent seasonal calendars or yield data.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Organized, no-nonsense structure mirrors how growers plan around seasons and margins.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple top nav with plain labels (Products, Seasons, Suppliers, Contact) and no jargon.
- cta_style: Solid, high-contrast buttons ("Request a Quote", "Get Growing Guide") that read as direct and useful.
- psychology: Straightforward interaction respects a busy, results-driven audience with little patience for gloss.

### Copy Style
- tone: Plainspoken, credible, rooted in expertise.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Concrete language about yield, cost, and reliability builds trust faster than aspirational marketing speak.
