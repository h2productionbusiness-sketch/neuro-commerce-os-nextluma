# Cybersecurity Doctrine
business_type: cybersecurity
sector: Technology & SaaS

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0D1B2A"
- secondary: "#132F4C"
- accent: "#2ECC71"
- psychology: Fortress midnight-blue projects vigilance and institutional trust, while a signal green accent maps to "secure / protected" status cues buyers already associate with safety.

### Typography
- heading: Roboto Condensed (Sans-serif)
- body: Roboto (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A tight, disciplined sans reads as controlled and authoritative — the visual equivalent of a locked-down system.

### Imagery
- style: Shield and lock motifs, threat-map dashboards, SOC control-room visuals, and clean data-flow diagrams.
- subjects: Security dashboards, encrypted flows, compliance badges, and calm analysts in control rather than chaos.
- psychology: Images should trigger reassurance and controlled confidence — the feeling of a threat neutralized before it lands.

### Layout
- style: Structured enterprise layout with threat proof points, compliance logos, and clear ROI/risk sections.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Orderly, evidence-dense structure signals the rigor and discipline buyers expect from a security vendor.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Enterprise top nav with Platform, Compliance, and a "Request a demo" plus "Talk to security" path.
- cta_style: Confident, low-hype CTAs ("Request a demo", "Get a risk assessment") that respect a cautious buyer.
- psychology: Calm, consultative CTAs avoid the false urgency that erodes trust with risk-averse decision-makers.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authoritative, reassuring, evidence-led.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Clear proof of protection and compliance converts fear of breach into confidence in the vendor.
