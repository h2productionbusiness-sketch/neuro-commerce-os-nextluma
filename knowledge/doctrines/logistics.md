# Logistics & Supply Chain Doctrine
business_type: logistics
sector: Automotive & Transport

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#12294D"
- secondary: "#EEF1F6"
- accent: "#FFB020"
- psychology: Deep navy signals enterprise dependability and global scale, cool grey reads as systematic clarity, and amber-gold accents evoke movement, on-time delivery, and cargo-in-motion signaling.

### Typography
- heading: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- body: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A single robust technical family reads as precise, integrated, and enterprise-grade, mirroring a supply chain where every link is standardized.

### Imagery
- style: Wide operational photography with clean data-overlay motifs and aerial logistics scale.
- subjects: Ports, fleets in motion, warehouse robotics, route maps, and real-time tracking dashboards.
- psychology: Images should trigger confidence in scale, reassurance of visibility and control, and trust in on-time reliability.

### Layout
- style: Structured dashboard-inspired grid with KPI cards, timelines, and route visualizations.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Data-forward structure signals operational command and end-to-end visibility, the core buying trigger for supply-chain decision-makers.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Top nav with solutions-by-role and a prominent "Track" and "Get a Quote" utility.
- cta_style: Confident rectangular buttons ("Request a Quote", "Book a Demo", "Track Shipment") in navy with amber highlight.
- psychology: Direct outcome-driven CTAs match a B2B buyer who values efficiency and hard ROI over persuasion flourish.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authoritative, metrics-driven, operationally precise.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Quantified proof (on-time %, cost-per-mile, transit days) reduces perceived risk in high-stakes procurement decisions.
