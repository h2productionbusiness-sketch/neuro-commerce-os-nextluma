# Physical Retail Doctrine
business_type: retail
sector: Retail & E-commerce

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#B02E3A"
- secondary: "#FBF7F0"
- accent: "#2E7D5B"
- psychology: A warm brick-red draws footfall and signals sales energy, a soft cream backdrop feels welcoming and tactile, and a fresh green accent conveys value and in-stock availability.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Source Sans 3 (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Rounded, friendly sans headlines feel approachable and local while a clean body face keeps signage and shelf-talkers readable at a glance.

### Imagery
- style: Warm, natural-light store and product photography with a human, neighborhood feel.
- subjects: Storefronts, staff and customers interacting, merchandised displays, and local community moments.
- psychology: Images should trigger belonging, the comfort of a trusted local place, and the pull of in-person discovery.

### Layout
- style: Location-forward layout with store hours, map, and promotions above the fold.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Making the physical presence obvious reduces friction for a visit and reinforces that this is a real place you can walk into today.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Prominent store locator, hours, and click-and-collect entry points in the header.
- cta_style: Action buttons like "Get Directions", "Reserve In-Store", and "See Today's Deals".
- psychology: Bridging digital intent to physical visits makes the next step concrete and low-effort.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, local, and community-minded.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Neighborly language and local references build familiarity and the sense of supporting a place that belongs to the community.
