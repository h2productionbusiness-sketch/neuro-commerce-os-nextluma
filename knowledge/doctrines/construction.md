# Construction & Contracting Doctrine
business_type: construction
sector: Real Estate & Construction

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A1D21"
- secondary: "#D9DCE1"
- accent: "#F4B400"
- psychology: Industrial charcoal signals structural strength and durability, steel grey conveys engineered precision, and safety-yellow cues jobsite competence and craftsmanship that reassures clients the build will stand.

### Typography
- heading: Oswald (Sans-serif)
- body: Roboto (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Condensed structural headlines feel solid and load-bearing while a mechanical body signals blueprint-level accuracy and reliability.

### Imagery
- style: Documentary jobsite photography with cranes, framing, and finished builds shot in strong daylight.
- subjects: Crews in hard hats, timelapse builds, detailed craftsmanship, and completed commercial and custom homes.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust in capability, pride of craft, and confidence that the project will finish on time and on spec.

### Layout
- style: Portfolio-led layout with large project galleries, phased timeline blocks, and credential badges.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A strong visual project portfolio does the persuading — clients buy proof of finished work more than promises.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Top nav featuring Projects, Services, Process, and Licenses with a bold consultation CTA.
- cta_style: Strong solid buttons ("Request a Bid", "Schedule a Consultation") reinforced by license and bonding badges.
- psychology: Confident CTAs backed by credentials lower the perceived risk of committing to a large, high-cost build.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, credentialed, process-driven.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Specific talk of timelines, licensing, and process reassures clients that execution is systematic, not improvised.
