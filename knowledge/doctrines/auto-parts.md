# Auto Parts & Repair Doctrine
business_type: auto-parts
sector: Automotive & Transport

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1C2733"
- secondary: "#E8EAED"
- accent: "#F2680C"
- psychology: Gunmetal steel-blue signals mechanical toughness and reliability, cool grey reads as clean garage precision, and safety-orange accents drive urgency and grab attention like a warning beacon.

### Typography
- heading: Rajdhani (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Wide industrial condensed headlines feel like stamped-metal signage and rugged capability while a neutral body reads as trustworthy spec-sheet clarity.

### Imagery
- style: High-contrast photography of parts, engine bays, and hands-on repair with gritty realistic lighting.
- subjects: Close-ups of components, torque wrenches on bolts, before/after fixes, and confident mechanics in the shop.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust in competence, relief that the problem is solvable, and confidence in getting back on the road.

### Layout
- style: Dense grid with prominent fitment filters, spec tables, and clear part-number hierarchy.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Organized density signals a deep catalog and expert command of every part, reassuring buyers they will find the exact fit.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent vehicle-selector (year/make/model) filter bar with category mega-menu.
- cta_style: Bold blocky buttons ("Check Fitment", "Add to Cart", "Book Repair") in high-contrast orange.
- psychology: Removing fitment doubt with a fixed selector eliminates the primary purchase anxiety and accelerates the decision.

### Copy Style
- tone: Straightforward, knowledgeable, no-nonsense mechanic-to-driver.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Plain specific language proves expertise and reduces the fear of buying the wrong part or being upsold.
