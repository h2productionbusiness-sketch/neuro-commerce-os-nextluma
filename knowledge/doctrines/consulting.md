# Management Consulting Doctrine
business_type: consulting
sector: B2B & Professional Services

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0A2540"
- secondary: "#F4F6F9"
- accent: "#1C6DD0"
- psychology: Deep navy signals institutional authority and trust, cool grey conveys analytical neutrality, and a confident blue accent reads as clarity and decisiveness for risk-averse enterprise buyers.

### Typography
- heading: Georgia (Serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A measured serif headline projects establishment credibility and gravitas while a clean neutral body reads as rigorous, data-driven objectivity.

### Imagery
- style: Documentary boardroom photography with natural light, plus clean data visualizations and framework diagrams.
- subjects: Senior executives in discussion, strategy whiteboards, charts, and abstract geometric frameworks.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, competence, and the confidence that complex problems are being handled by serious experts.

### Layout
- style: Structured multi-column grid with clear hierarchy, framework blocks, and evidence callouts.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Orderly structure signals disciplined thinking and reduces perceived risk of engaging an expensive advisor.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent top navigation with clear service pillars and an insights/thought-leadership hub.
- cta_style: Confident solid-button CTAs ("Book a Consultation", "Request Proposal") paired with credibility proof points.
- psychology: Clear, direct interaction design mirrors the decisiveness clients are paying the firm to provide.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authoritative, precise, insight-led.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Evidence-backed language reassures buyers that recommendations are grounded in rigor, not opinion.
