# Breweries & Distilleries Doctrine
business_type: brewery
sector: Food & Beverage

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#3B2417"
- secondary: "#E8DCC0"
- accent: "#C4471C"
- psychology: Toasted-malt brown grounds the brand in barrel and grain craft, warm wheat-cream evokes raw ingredients and honesty, and copper-kettle rust signals hands-on artisanal brewing heritage.

### Typography
- heading: Alfa Slab One (Slab-serif)
- body: Work Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Chunky slab-serif type reads like vintage label and taproom craft, projecting rugged authenticity and small-batch pride.

### Imagery
- style: Textured craft photography with copper tanks, grain, hop cones, and taproom pours.
- subjects: Brewers at the tanks, foamy pours, barrel rooms, and friends gathered in the taproom.
- psychology: Images should trigger appreciation for craft, authenticity, and the communal warmth of the taproom.

### Layout
- style: Rugged grid with strong labels, batch stories, and a taproom-forward feel.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A textured, label-driven layout mirrors the tactile craft product and rewards curiosity about each batch.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sturdy top bar with Beers, Taproom, Tours, and Shop.
- cta_style: Bold blocky buttons ("Visit the Taproom", "Shop the Release", "Book a Tour").
- psychology: Solid confident CTAs match the grounded, no-nonsense character of craft brewing culture.

### Copy Style
- tone: Authentic, passionate, craft-nerd friendly.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Detailed talk of process, hops, and barrels signals genuine craft and converts casual drinkers into devotees.
