# Publishing Doctrine
business_type: publishing
sector: Entertainment & Media

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#2B2118"
- secondary: "#F4ECD8"
- accent: "#8B5E34"
- psychology: Ink-brown grounds the brand in the tactile authority of print, aged-paper cream evokes the warmth and trust of a beloved book, and leather-tan cues heritage craftsmanship and literary permanence.

### Typography
- heading: Playfair Display (Serif)
- body: Lora (Serif)
- psychology: High-contrast literary serifs read as bookish authority and editorial taste while a readable text serif honors long-form reading comfort and the ritual of turning pages.

### Imagery
- style: Warm still-life photography of books and reading nooks, author portraits, and typographic cover art.
- subjects: Cover reveals, stacked spines, hands holding books, cozy reading scenes, and author at work.
- psychology: Images should trigger nostalgia, intellectual belonging, and the intimate anticipation of a story about to unfold.

### Layout
- style: Editorial magazine grid with generous margins, pull-quotes, and cover-forward feature blocks.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Breathing room and typographic hierarchy signal editorial quality and invite slow, considered reading rather than skimming.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Clean top bar with Books, Authors, Excerpts, and Newsletter, prioritizing browse and sample.
- cta_style: Restrained buttons ("Read an Excerpt", "Pre-order", "Join the Newsletter") that lead with sampling before buying.
- psychology: Sample-first CTAs lower risk and let the writing earn the purchase, matching how readers actually decide.

### Copy Style
- tone: Literary, warm, quietly persuasive.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Crafted, voice-rich copy demonstrates the quality of the writing itself and builds trust with a discerning reader.
