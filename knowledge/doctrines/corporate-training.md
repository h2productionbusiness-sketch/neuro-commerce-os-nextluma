## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#12324A"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#2FB6A3"
- psychology: Executive slate-blue projects competence and B2B credibility, clean white signals clarity and efficiency, and teal accents suggest growth and measurable transformation without feeling frivolous.

### Typography
- heading: Inter (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A single precise, neutral grotesque family reads as modern, systematic, and enterprise-ready, reinforcing that the program is rigorous and results-driven.

### Imagery
- style: Clean corporate photography and data-forward illustration with confident, diverse professionals.
- subjects: Facilitated workshops, collaborative teams, dashboards showing skill gains, and speakers at scale.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, competence, and the anticipation of tangible workforce ROI.

### Layout
- style: Modular grid with outcome stats, logo walls, and skills-framework diagrams.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Structured, evidence-dense layout mirrors the analytical mindset of L&D and procurement buyers evaluating vendors.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Clear solution-oriented nav segmented by role, industry, and outcome with a prominent demo CTA.
- cta_style: Direct value-led buttons ("Book a Demo", "Download ROI Guide", "Talk to Sales") with supporting proof points.
- psychology: Low-friction lead-capture CTAs match a considered B2B buying committee that needs to justify the purchase internally.

### Copy Style
- tone: Confident, pragmatic, ROI- and outcome-driven.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Evidence-backed, jargon-fluent language builds credibility with L&D leaders while quantified outcomes de-risk the internal business case.
