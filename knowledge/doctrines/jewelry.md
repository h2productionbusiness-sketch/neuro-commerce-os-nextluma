# Jewelry Doctrine
business_type: jewelry
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1A1712"
- secondary: "#FBF7F0"
- accent: "#B8912F"
- psychology: Warm near-black velvet evokes the jewelry box and heirloom permanence, soft champagne cream flatters metal and skin, and antique gold signals lasting value over trend.

### Typography
- heading: Cormorant Garamond (Serif)
- body: Jost (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A delicate high-contrast serif feels precious and timeless while an airy geometric body keeps care and provenance details clear.

### Imagery
- style: Macro jewelry photography with soft directional light, on-skin close-ups, and hero-on-velvet shots.
- subjects: Single pieces on skin and stone, sparkle and facet macros, hands and necklines, meaningful moments.
- psychology: Images should trigger reverence, emotional attachment to milestones, and the sense of enduring worth.

### Layout
- style: Elegant editorial grid with generous framing and provenance and craft storytelling blocks.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Spacious framing signals preciousness and slows the buyer into a considered, emotional decision.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Refined minimal nav with occasion, metal, and stone filters plus an appointment or consultation entry.
- cta_style: Understated elegant buttons ("Discover the Piece", "Book a Consultation") with trust and guarantee cues.
- psychology: Consultative, low-pressure interaction builds the trust a high-consideration purchase demands.

### Copy Style
- tone: Timeless, emotional, provenance-led.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Story and meaning language ties the piece to identity and milestones, justifying premium price.
