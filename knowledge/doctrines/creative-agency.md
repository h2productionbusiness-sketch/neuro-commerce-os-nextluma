# Creative Agencies Doctrine
business_type: creative-agency
sector: B2B & Professional Services

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#111114"
- secondary: "#FAFAF7"
- accent: "#FF4D2E"
- psychology: Near-black canvas signals confident craft and lets work take center stage, warm off-white breathes gallery calm, and a vivid vermilion accent reads as bold creative energy that dares clients to be seen.

### Typography
- heading: Clash Display (Sans-serif)
- body: Satoshi (Sans-serif)
- psychology: An expressive geometric display voice signals originality and boldness while a clean modern body keeps the portfolio feeling contemporary and self-assured.

### Imagery
- style: High-impact portfolio photography, motion stills, and case-study mockups with vibrant color and bold cropping.
- subjects: Award-winning campaign work, behind-the-scenes creative process, and striking brand transformations.
- psychology: Images should trigger surprise, aspiration, and the belief that this partner will make the client unforgettable.

### Layout
- style: Bold asymmetric editorial grid with oversized case-study reels and dynamic scroll motion.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Confident emptiness and dramatic scale signal that the agency's taste is the product being sold.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Minimal top bar with a prominent Work index and playful hover-driven micro-interactions.
- cta_style: Bold, personality-driven CTAs ("Let's Make Something", "Start a Project") with animated hover states.
- psychology: Expressive interaction design demonstrates creative capability before a word of copy is read.

### Copy Style
- tone: Bold, witty, confidently creative.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy, distinctive language proves the agency can craft a voice worth paying for.
