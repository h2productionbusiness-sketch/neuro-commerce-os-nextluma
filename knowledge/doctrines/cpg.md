# CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods) Doctrine
business_type: cpg
sector: Retail & E-commerce

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0E5C4A"
- secondary: "#FFFDF5"
- accent: "#F2B705"
- psychology: A grounded forest green signals natural, clean, and trustworthy ingredients, an off-white shelf-canvas reads fresh and hygienic, and a sunny yellow accent pops on-shelf to win the split-second grab decision.

### Typography
- heading: Recoleta (Serif)
- body: Work Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: A soft rounded serif feels wholesome and appetizing while a neutral sans keeps ingredient panels and claims clear and compliant.

### Imagery
- style: Appetizing, texture-rich product and ingredient photography with clean styling.
- subjects: The product in use, raw ingredients, packaging on-shelf, and lifestyle moments of consumption.
- psychology: Images should trigger appetite, trust in what's inside, and the reassurance of a wholesome everyday choice.

### Layout
- style: Packaging-forward layout emphasizing product benefits, claims, and where-to-buy.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Clear hierarchy of claims and benefits mirrors shelf decision-making, letting shoppers justify the pick in seconds.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Simple product-and-benefit nav with a prominent "Where to Buy" and retailer links.
- cta_style: Buttons like "Find in Store", "Buy on [Retailer]", and "Get Coupons".
- psychology: Routing intent to retail and coupons meets CPG buyers where they actually purchase and lowers trial friction.

### Copy Style
- tone: Wholesome, benefit-led, and trustworthy.
- length: Short
- psychology: Punchy claims and clean benefit statements match low-consideration, habit-driven purchase behavior.
