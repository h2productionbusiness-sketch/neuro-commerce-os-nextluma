# Cosmetics & Skincare Doctrine
business_type: cosmetics
sector: Fashion & Beauty

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#E8B4B8"
- secondary: "#FFFDFB"
- accent: "#7A5C58"
- psychology: Soft rose-nude signals gentle self-care and skin positivity, near-white cream reads clean and dermatologically safe, and a muted mocha accent adds grounded, science-backed credibility.

### Typography
- heading: Playfair Display (Serif)
- body: Nunito Sans (Sans-serif)
- psychology: An elegant editorial serif feels indulgent and beauty-magazine premium while a soft rounded body reads approachable and reassuring for claims.

### Imagery
- style: Clean beauty photography with soft light, glowing skin texture, and ingredient and swatch macros.
- subjects: Diverse real skin close-ups, before-and-after and texture swatches, ingredients, routine and ritual moments.
- psychology: Images should trigger aspiration toward glow, trust in results, and the calm of a self-care ritual.

### Layout
- style: Clean routine-led grid with ingredient callouts, results proof, and before-after blocks.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Airy structure feels clinical-clean while proof blocks build the trust efficacy claims require.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Concern and routine-based nav with a skin quiz and ingredient glossary.
- cta_style: Soft rounded buttons ("Build My Routine", "Take the Skin Quiz") with review and result cues.
- psychology: A guided quiz personalizes the journey and reduces choice anxiety in a claims-heavy category.

### Copy Style
- tone: Warm, science-backed, empowering.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Balancing evidence with self-love language earns trust while tying results to identity and confidence.
