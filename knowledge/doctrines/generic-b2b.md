## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#1B2A4A"
- secondary: "#F4F6F9"
- accent: "#2F80ED"
- psychology: Deep navy signals stability and institutional trust, a cool light-grey canvas reads as clean and professional, and a confident blue accent implies competence and forward momentum without emotional volatility.

### Typography
- heading: Inter (Sans-serif)
- body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Neutral, highly legible grotesques convey clarity and reliability, reassuring risk-averse procurement buyers that the offering is rational and dependable.

### Imagery
- style: Clean corporate photography and simple line-based diagrams with product screenshots.
- subjects: Teams collaborating, dashboards and data visualizations, real people using the product in context.
- psychology: Images should trigger trust, competence, and the sense of a low-risk, proven solution.

### Layout
- style: Structured multi-column grid with clear hierarchy, benefit blocks, and proof sections.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Organized, scannable structure signals operational maturity and reduces perceived decision risk for committee buyers.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Persistent top navigation with clear product, solutions, pricing, and resources sections.
- cta_style: Solid primary buttons ("Request a Demo", "Talk to Sales") paired with a lower-commitment secondary link.
- psychology: Predictable, conventional patterns lower cognitive friction and let evaluators focus on evaluating capability, not learning the interface.

### Copy Style
- tone: Clear, credible, benefit- and outcome-focused.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Specific, quantified claims backed by proof reduce uncertainty and give internal champions language to sell upward.
