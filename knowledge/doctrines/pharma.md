# Pharma & Biotech Doctrine
business_type: pharma
sector: Health & Wellness

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#123A6B"
- secondary: "#EEF2F6"
- accent: "#00B3A4"
- psychology: Deep institutional navy signals scientific authority, regulatory rigor, and trust, cool light grey reads as clean-room precision, and a teal accent injects innovation and life-science vitality.

### Typography
- heading: IBM Plex Sans (Sans-serif)
- body: IBM Plex Serif (Serif)
- psychology: An engineered sans headline conveys technical credibility while a measured serif body lends the gravity of peer-reviewed evidence.

### Imagery
- style: Precise scientific and clinical photography with laboratory detail, data visualization, and controlled lighting.
- subjects: Researchers at benches, molecular and mechanism-of-action visuals, clinical settings, and clean data charts.
- psychology: Images should trigger confidence in rigor, credibility of the science, and reassurance of safety and evidence.

### Layout
- style: Structured, evidence-dense layout with clear data hierarchy, references, and compliant disclosures.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: Orderly, citation-rich structure signals regulatory seriousness and satisfies scrutinizing scientific and payer audiences.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Formal top navigation segmented by audience (HCP, patient, investor) with clear compliance footers.
- cta_style: Measured CTAs ("Request data", "Contact medical affairs", "View the evidence") without hype.
- psychology: Restrained, audience-gated CTAs respect compliance and match the deliberate, high-stakes decision process.

### Copy Style
- tone: Precise, authoritative, evidence-anchored, compliant.
- length: Long
- psychology: Thorough, cited language builds credibility with expert buyers who distrust marketing shortcuts.
