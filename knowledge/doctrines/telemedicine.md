# Telemedicine Doctrine
business_type: telemedicine
sector: Health & Wellness

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#0E9CA8"
- secondary: "#FFFFFF"
- accent: "#3D6EF2"
- psychology: Fresh medical teal reads as modern, clean, and caring, crisp white conveys clinical hygiene and clarity, and a confident blue accent signals technological trust and reliability.

### Typography
- heading: Poppins (Sans-serif)
- body: Inter (Sans-serif)
- psychology: Friendly geometric headlines make digital care feel human and accessible while a neutral body face keeps instructions and pricing unambiguous.

### Imagery
- style: Clean, bright product-and-lifestyle photography blending real people with clear app UI screenshots.
- subjects: People consulting a doctor on a phone at home, smiling clinicians on video, simple device mockups, and step-by-step flows.
- psychology: Images should trigger convenience, relief at fast access, and confidence that remote care is legitimate and easy.

### Layout
- style: Conversion-focused landing structure with clear steps, proof, and pricing above the fold.
- white_space: Moderate
- psychology: A guided, step-numbered layout reduces friction anxiety and shows how simple getting care will be.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Sticky header with a persistent "Book a visit" or "Start now" CTA and visible trust signals.
- cta_style: Prominent action CTAs ("See a doctor in minutes", "Get started") reinforced by wait-time and licensing badges.
- psychology: Immediacy-focused CTAs convert acute-need visitors who value speed over deliberation.

### Copy Style
- tone: Clear, reassuring, efficient, professional.
- length: Short
- psychology: Concise plain language removes friction for symptomatic users who want an answer, not a lecture.
