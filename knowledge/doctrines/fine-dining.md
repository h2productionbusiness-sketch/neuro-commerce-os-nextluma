# Fine Dining Doctrine
business_type: fine-dining
sector: Food & Beverage

## Visual Doctrine

### Colors
- primary: "#141210"
- secondary: "#EDE4D3"
- accent: "#8C6A3F"
- psychology: Warm near-black evokes candlelit intimacy and occasion, aged parchment cream signals heritage and craft, and burnished bronze suggests understated luxury without gaudiness.

### Typography
- heading: Cormorant Garamond (Serif)
- body: Freight Text (Serif)
- psychology: Elegant high-contrast serifs read as culinary artistry and tradition, telling the diner this is a curated experience rather than a transaction.

### Imagery
- style: Moody chiaroscuro food photography with shallow depth of field and natural light on dark surfaces.
- subjects: Plated tasting-menu courses, chef's hands at the pass, sommelier pours, and intimate dining-room ambience.
- psychology: Images should trigger anticipation, sensory desire, and the reverence of witnessing craftsmanship.

### Layout
- style: Editorial single-column storytelling with oversized plating photography and sparse elegant captions.
- white_space: Generous
- psychology: Breathing room around each dish frames the meal as art, slowing the eye and elevating perceived value.

### UI Patterns
- navigation: Minimal hairline top bar with Reservations, Menu, and Story, no promotional clutter.
- cta_style: Refined text-link CTAs ("Reserve a Table", "View the Tasting Menu") with subtle underlines.
- psychology: Quiet, deliberate interactions mirror the unhurried pacing of a fine dining service.

### Copy Style
- tone: Poetic, provenance-driven, sensory.
- length: Medium
- psychology: Evocative language about ingredients and origin makes diners taste the meal before they arrive, justifying the price.
